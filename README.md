## Ranking System
The ranks are reflected on a couple of criteria and are meant to help more accurately display your skill (especially when comparing it to the map you played). Here is how each rank works:

S: Accuracy of 98%+ and 7 or less combo breaks <br>
A: Accuracy of 95%+ and 15 or less combo breaks <br>
B: Accuracy of 90%+ and a loss of less than 1000 health <br>
C: Accuracy of 85%+ and a loss of less than 3000 health <br>
D: Accuracy of 80%+ and a loss of less than 6000 health <br>
F: A failiure to reach 80%+ Accuracy or losing more than 6000 pts of health

In addition to these, three subranks are also in the calculator:

All Perfect: Every note is a perfect <br>
Full Combo: No combo breaks are detected (Perfects or Greats) <br>
Mastery: When 7 or less combo breaks are detected (Think about unlocking the master difficulty of a chart)

## Health
Health is a hidden mechanic of the calculator. It starts from 0 and decreased based on the amount of health you were estimated to lose during the chart. Any bad input will result in -40 HP and any miss will result in -75HP. In the game, the calculations are a bit different as slider misses are less penilizing as missing a note is. You can check that here.
https://www.sekaipedia.org/wiki/Rhythm_game_mechanics#Judgement

## Accuracy
Accuracy is also based on the in-game score calculation. Perfects give 100%, Greats give 70%, and Goods give 50%. Missing or hitting a note badly results in 0% accuracy.
